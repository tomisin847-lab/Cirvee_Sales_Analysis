# Customer Segmentation & Behavior Analysis (Excel)

## Project Overview
This project analyzes customer purchase data to understand customer behavior,
identify high-value customers, and segment customers based on spending and
purchase frequency.

The goal is to provide actionable insights that a business can use to improve
customer retention and revenue.

---

## Business Questions
- Who are the best customers?
- How often do customers purchase?
- Which customers are loyal vs one-time buyers?
- Which customer segments generate the most revenue?

---

## Dataset
The dataset contains customer purchase records including:
- Customer ID
- Order ID
- Order Date
- Product
- Quantity
- Unit Price
- Total Amount

The raw data was messy and required cleaning before analysis.

---

## Data Cleaning Process
The following steps were performed in Excel:

1. Converted raw data into Excel Tables
2. Trimmed text columns to remove extra spaces
3. Standardized mixed date formats
4. Handled missing quantities by assuming 1 unit
5. Recalculated total sales to ensure accuracy
6. Created helper columns (Month, Year)
7. Removed errors and validated values


---

## Analysis Steps (customize this to your own analysis)
1. Created a customer-level summary table to segment customers based on their value and purchasing frequency.
2. Calculated:
   - Total Spend per Customer
   - Purchase Frequency
   - Average Order Value
3. Segmented customers into:
   - High, Medium, and Low Value
   - Loyal, Occasional, and One-Time
4. Built Pivot Tables to analyze:
   - Customer distribution
   - Revenue by segment
   - Customer behavior trends

## Visualizations (customize this to your actual visuals)

## Key Insights
- A large proportion of customers actively contribute to overall revenue, indicating a broad and healthy customer base rather than reliance on a few individuals.
- Lagos State generates the highest share of total revenue, making it the companyâ€™s strongest and most profitable market.
- Customer retention is strong, as a significant number of customers make repeat purchases over time.
- Customers who purchase multiple times tend to have higher total spend, highlighting the value of retaining existing customers.
- The business should continue investing in retention strategies while leveraging its strong presence in Lagos to drive further growth.
